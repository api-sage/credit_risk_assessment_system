What is a Credit Score?
Your credit score is a three-digit reflection of your financial history, indicating your reliability in managing money and repaying debts. Influenced by various factors such as credit history, loan duration, and payment punctuality, it serves as a measure of your trustworthiness as a borrower.
Good vs Bad Credit Score
A strong credit score signals to lenders that you're a reliable borrower, increasing your chances of securing loans, credit cards, and favorable interest rates. Conversely, a poor credit score, typically below 630 on a scale of 300-850, may limit your borrowing options and lead to less favorable terms.
